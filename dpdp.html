DPDP Compliance

Our statement of compliance with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, describing how Dropt processes personal data lawfully, securely and with respect for the rights of data principals.

Last updated: 2025

1. Our Commitment

Skylink Innovation ("Dropt") is committed to protecting the personal data of every individual who interacts with our hyperlocal delivery platform. We process personal data in accordance with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (the "DPDP Act") and the rules made under it. This statement explains how we meet our obligations under the DPDP Act and supplements our Privacy Policy.

2. Our Role

Under the DPDP Act, Dropt acts as a Data Fiduciary in respect of personal data we determine the purpose and means of processing — for example, data relating to our own users and delivery partners. Where we process personal data on behalf of a brand customer to operate their deliveries, we act as a Data Processor under the instructions of that brand, who remains the Data Fiduciary for that data. Our responsibilities are defined accordingly under each role.

3. Lawful Processing & Consent

We process personal data only for lawful purposes and on a valid legal basis, which is typically the free, specific, informed and unambiguous consent of the data principal, or a legitimate use permitted under the DPDP Act. Where processing relies on consent, we provide a clear notice of the personal data being collected and the purpose of processing, and we enable data principals to withdraw their consent as easily as it was given.

4. Purpose Limitation

We collect and process personal data only for the specific purposes for which it was provided, and we do not use it for incompatible purposes without an appropriate legal basis. We limit collection to what is necessary to deliver our services, and we retain personal data only for as long as required to fulfil those purposes or to comply with legal obligations.

5. Data Principal Rights

In line with the DPDP Act, data principals have the right to access a summary of their personal data and how it is processed, the right to correction and updating of inaccurate or incomplete data, and the right to erasure of personal data that is no longer required. Data principals may also nominate another individual to exercise their rights in the event of death or incapacity, and have the right to readily available grievance redressal. We provide accessible means to exercise each of these rights.

6. Security Safeguards

We implement reasonable technical and organisational security safeguards to protect personal data, including encryption in transit, access controls, tenant isolation between brands, role-based permissions, audit logging and regular reviews. These measures are designed to prevent personal data breaches and to maintain the confidentiality, integrity and availability of the data we process.

7. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will take prompt action to contain and remediate the incident and will notify the Data Protection Board of India and affected data principals in the manner and within the timelines prescribed under the DPDP Act. Where we act as a Data Processor, we will inform the relevant Data Fiduciary without undue delay so that they can meet their own notification obligations.

8. Cross-Border Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside India — for example, to cloud or service providers — we do so only in accordance with the DPDP Act and any restrictions notified by the Central Government, and under appropriate contractual safeguards designed to protect the data to the standard required by Indian law.

9. Grievance Officer

We have appointed a Grievance Officer to address questions, concerns and complaints regarding the processing of personal data and the exercise of data principal rights. Data principals may contact our Grievance Officer at grievance@dropt.in. Skylink Innovation is based in Bangalore, Karnataka, India. We will acknowledge and respond to grievances within the timelines prescribed by law.

10. Updates

We may update this DPDP Compliance statement from time to time to reflect changes in the law, the rules made under the DPDP Act, or our practices. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date and, where appropriate, provide additional notice.
